Crepes

Description

A crêpe or crepe is a dish made from unleavened batter or dough that is cooked on a frying pan or a griddle. Crêpes are usually one of two varieties: sweet crêpes or savoury galettes. .

Ingridients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk, or more as needed
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter (melted)

Steps

  1. Whisk eggs, milk, water, and salt together in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add flour and melted butter.
  3. Whisk vigorously until batter is smooth and pourable.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the pan, using approximately 1/4 cup for each crêpe.
  5. Cook until the top of the crêpe is no longer wet and the bottom has turned light brown, 1 to 2 minutes. Run a spatula around the edge of the skillet to loosen the crêpe; flip and cook until the other side has turned light brown, about 1 minute more.
